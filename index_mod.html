  • Introduction to the Lego Group

    This year The Lego Group celebrates our 10 year anniversary with the United Nations Global Compact, showing our long-term commitment of being a responsible manufacturer of play experiences.In the LEGO Group everything is about making children's play with LEGO toys as positive an experience as possible. We believe this is best done through high-quality products. The founder of the company Ole Kirk Kristiansen was determined that his company should always provide children with products where only the best is good enough.

    It is our mission to 'Inspire and develop the builders of tomorrow', a reflection of our fundamental belief in the value of play and learning in society and for children in particular. Through this mission we serve the company's ultimate purpose to inspire and develop children to think creatively, reason systematically and work collaboratively to release their potential to shape their own future. As a consequence of this it is natural for us to stand up for, and ensure, children's right to play.

What Our Stakeholders Tell Us

Stake holders came to us with three main concerns:

  1. Safety and quality of products
  2. Supporting children's right to grow
  3. Communication with children
Safety and Quality of Products

Always the Safest Products.

    The LEGO Group operates in the EU toy category market that saw a total of 366 safety notifications in 2013. We are proud to announce that we had no safety notifications globally for the third year in a row, thereby adhering to our zero product recall expectation.
A Child's Right To Grow

Continued focus on our social impact has led us to develop a Human Rights approach, which takes
outset in Children's Rights and Business principles at the centre.

  1. Meet the responsibility to respect and commit to supporting children's human rights
  2. Contribute towards the elimination of child labour, including all business activities and relationships
  3. Provide decent work for young workers, parents and caregivers
  4. Ensure the protection and safety of children in all business activities and facilities
  5. Ensure that products and services are safe, and seek to support children's rights through them
  6. Use marketing and advertising that respect and support children's rights
Communication With Children

The LEGO Group believes in learning through play, any type of play. Free play can sometimes be forgotten,
but it is key to unleashing our creative skills.

    Play invites excitement and stimulates social and cognitive skills. It fosters creative and critical thinking. By engaging in learning through play, children grow into more confident, curious and resourceful individuals. We hope that by inspiring and encouraging children to challenge their creative flair at an early stage, they will contribute to a smarter and more innovative tomorrow.

Building A Responsible Business

LEGO products for all children

Our range embraces products for all children, i.e. "children at all ages". The entire product portfolio is graduated in its challenge to reflect the fact that children grow older and develop. LEGO products can be grouped into a number of categories, including LEGO bricks, LEGO educational products, LEGO video games, and LEGO play themes, and LEGO media which includes our Youtube channel that has over 1 million views.

Gender and toys

Our aim is to ensure that we apply sufficient gender awareness in our product design and marketing development. All children are unique, and each of them has a range of interests.

Our new Gender Marketing Guidelines underline how to strike a balance when creating communication and products that appeal to both boys and girls. Furthermore, it emphasises that the LEGO Group is committed to ensuring that children are not being subjected to, or limited by, gender stereotypes.

Responsibility areas that echo our promises

Guided by our four brand promises, we embed responsibility at the heart of all our operations to constantly ensure we adhere to stakeholder expectations and the United Nations Global Compact. To measure our environmental, social and governance impact, we have anchored responsibility areas into our four promises.

The Play Promise makes sure anyone who uses our products shares in the joy of building and the pride of creation with the mutual value Partner Promise that takes into account a positive impact Planet Promise so we can accomplish the People Promise and succeed together.

Managing energy and waste efficiently

In 2013, we achieved a very satisfactory energy efficiency improvement of 6.7%, accomplishing an improvement of more than 30% over a five year period.

As 10% of the LEGO Group's CO2 emissions are linked to the packaging material we use, sustainable packaging is a focus area for us. In recent years, and in close collaboration with some of our main stakeholders, we have worked on reducing the size of our packaging by up to 18%.

We reached our target on energy efficiency in 2013. Having a total consumption of 247,000 GWh, we became 6.7% more energy efficient compared to the previous year. Such an improvement equals a reduction of more than 6,500 tonnes CO2.
